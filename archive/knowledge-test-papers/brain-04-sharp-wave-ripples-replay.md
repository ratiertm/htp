# Sharp Wave Ripples and Hippocampal Replay

Sharp wave ripples are high frequency neural oscillations originating in the
hippocampal CA3 region during quiet wakefulness and slow wave sleep. During these
events, sequences of place cell activity that occurred during prior experience are
reactivated at compressed timescales, a phenomenon termed hippocampal replay.

Replay events occur in forward and reverse directions, supporting memory
consolidation, planning, and credit assignment. Forward replay traces predicted
future paths while reverse replay binds recent outcomes to preceding action
sequences. The reactivation transfers information from hippocampus to neocortex
through coordinated thalamocortical spindles, gradually consolidating episodic
memories into distributed cortical representations. Priority based replay
preferentially reactivates high reward or surprising experiences, with novelty
weighted prioritization shaping which memories undergo consolidation. This offline
replay mechanism enables learning beyond direct experience through internal
simulation of past events.
